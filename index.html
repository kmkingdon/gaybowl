Acclimating to Colorado:

Denver is called the Mile High City for a reason, the altitude here is real, and so is its effect on your body.  Some of the factors you should consider during your visit include:

 

  • Dehydration – Denver is actually a high desert, and you will need to stay more hydrated than you’re used to, particularly when you’re playing football or drinking alcohol. Locals are known to take a bottle of water everywhere they go, we recommend you do the same.  Drink up!

  • Sun burn – Denver is a mile closer to the sun than your skin is likely used to, and our thinner air means that you will burn more quickly when you’re out in the sun. We highly recommend wearing lots of sunscreen, and re-applying often.  Lucky for you, our Gay Bowl host committee has arranged for special sunscreen boys/girls to take care of you at the fields.  Keep an eye out for them!

  • Thin air – You will notice that you know matter how good of shape you are in, you are going to feel more out of breathe and fatigue more quickly than you’re used to. Don’t worry, it’s normal, there’s not quite as much oxygen here, and it takes up to 6 months to fully acclimate. 

  • Effects of Alcohol – Yes, you will get drunker more quickly here than you do at home.  So please drink in moderation, be aware of your lowered tolerance, and drink water often to stay hydrated when you’re out on the town. 

 




Transportation:


RTD (Regional Transportation District):


Regional Transportation District is Denver’s public transit system. In Denver we have a system of buses, light rails, and a train (University of Colorado A line) that connects the airport to downtown. These service the greater Denver area including rides up to Boulder and even out to Evergreen. Also check out the Free Mall Ride, which travels from Union Station, through 16 th Street Mall, to Civic Center Park (Right by the Sheraton).



Website: http://www.rtd-denver.com

Bustang:


The Bustang is a coach bus service through the Colorado Department of Transportation. It has buses going as far North as Fort Collins, South as Colorado Springs, and West as Glenwood Springs. If you are looking to extend your trip and see what else Colorado has to offer, this is a perfect option for getting around.



Website: https://www.ridebustang.com



Cost: Fares vary ($5-28) depending on the route and distance traveled. See the website (https://www.ridebustang.com/fares-and-tickets) or download the Bustang app for more details

 

Uber/Lyft:


Uber and Lyft have taken the country by storm. It’s amazing with a few clicks in an app; a driver will pick you up and deliver you to whichever location you choose. Both require an app so download them from the App/Play store.


Website: https://www.uber.com ;   https://www.lyft.com ;


Cost: Varies by distance and driver availability.

 

B-Cycle:


Denver does have a bike share program! If you are looking to get a little extra cardio (as if playing flag football is not enough) you should check out our B-Cycles. These cruisers can be checked out daily for 24-hours between 5 am and midnight.



Website: https://www.denverbcycle.com



Cost: $9 per bike for 24-hours, must return bike to a dock every 30 minutes

 

Car2Go:


Going a little further than a bike can take you? How about Car2Go, one of our car share programs in Denver. Simply download the app and register. Then use the app to find the nearest car to use. When done, just park in a legal spot and leave the keys for the next person!



Website: https://www.car2go.com/US/en/denver/



Cost: Rates range from $7-$169 depending on time frame not including insurance and other fees

 

Maven:


Another car share program is Maven. With Maven you also download the app. The app helps unlock and start the cars. When you’re done, simple park the car at the station you found it and hit “End Trip”



Website: https://www.maven.com/us/city/denver-co.html#/station-locations



Cost: Rates range from $7-$9 per hour not including additional fees
